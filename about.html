Who We Are

Built on Compassion,
Driven by Purpose

Stackly NGO was founded in Salem, Tamil Nadu, with a single belief: every human being deserves a life of dignity and opportunity. What started as a small community kitchen now spans emergency relief, education, healthcare, and environmental programs across the region.

We don't just deliver aid — we build lasting relationships with the communities we serve, co-creating solutions that respect local wisdom and address systemic root causes.

Our Core Values

Six principles that shape how we work, who we partner with, and the future we're building together.

01

Compassion

We lead with empathy, treating every individual with the dignity and respect they inherently deserve, regardless of circumstance.

02

Transparency

Open books, honest reporting, and accountable governance are non-negotiable. Donors deserve to know exactly how their contributions work.

03

Action-Oriented

We are a rapid-response organization. When lives are at stake, we don't deliberate — we mobilize and deliver where it matters most.

04

Inclusion

No one is left out. We champion diversity and ensure our programs reach the most marginalized, the overlooked, and the underserved.

05

Sustainability

Every solution we design is built to last. We invest in systems, skills, and structures that communities can own and continue independently.

06

Partnership

We believe in the power of collaboration. Our work multiplies when government, business, and communities act as one unified force.

Our Journey

A Decade of Impact

From humble beginnings to a force for change — here are the milestones that define our story.

2012 — Founded

A Kitchen That Started It All

Stackly NGO began as a community kitchen in Salem, feeding 50 families during a local drought. Our founder Priya Krishnamurthy's simple act of compassion sparked a movement.

2015 — Expanded

Education Comes First

We launched our first mobile school program, bringing certified teachers to 8 remote villages. Over 400 children enrolled in their first year of formal education.

2018 — Crisis Response

Kerala Flood Relief Operation

Stackly deployed a 120-volunteer team to support flood-affected families, distributing over ₹2 crore in aid within 72 hours — earning our first national recognition.

2020 — Pandemic Response

COVID-19 Support Network

We built a rapid-response network that provided food, medicine, and mental health support to over 15,000 families across Tamil Nadu during the pandemic.

2024 — Global Recognition

UN Partnership & Scale-Up

Stackly NGO was recognized by UNICEF as a model community organization, and we expanded our programs to 48 villages, touching 50,000+ lives and counting.
